'''Siegfried Johannes Gottwald''' (30 March 1943 – 20 September 2015) was a German [[mathematician]], [[logic]]ian and [[history of science|historian of science]].<ref name=ULeipzig>{{cite web|url=http://www.sozphil.uni-leipzig.de/cm/philosophie/mitarbeiter/siegfried-gottwald/|title=Prof. Dr. Siegfried Gottwald, Nachruf|publisher=[[Leipzig University]], Institut für Philosophie|language=de|accessdate=25 September 2015}}</ref>

==Life and work==
Gottwald was born in [[Limbach-Oberfrohna|Limbach]], [[Saxony]] in 1943.  From 1961 to 1966, he studied mathematics at the [[University of Leipzig]], where he was awarded his doctor title in 1969 and his [[habilitation]] in 1977.<ref name=ULeipzig/>

He was [[tenure]]d [[professor]] of non-classical and mathematical logic at University of Leipzig where he taught from 1972 to his retirement in 2008.<ref name=ULeipzig/> His main research areas are [[fuzzy set]]s and fuzzy methodologies, [[many-valued logic]] and [[history of mathematics]].<ref name=ULeipzig/>

He published several books on many-valued logic, fuzzy sets and their applications, co-authored a textbook on [[calculus]] and a reader in the history of logic. He also contributed to the German biographical dictionary of mathematicians, ''Lexikon berühmter Mathematiker''.

Professor Gottwald was Vice-Dean of the Faculty of Social Sciences and Philosophy at University of Leipzig for several years. He was married with three children.

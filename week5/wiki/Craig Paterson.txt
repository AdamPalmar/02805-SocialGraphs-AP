'''Craig Paterson''' (born 2 October 1959 in [[South Queensferry]]) is a [[Scotland|Scottish]] former [[football (soccer)|football]] player, who currently works as a pundit for [[BBC Radio Scotland]] and ''[[Sportscene]]''.

The son of former [[Hibernian F.C.|Hibernian]] defender [[Jock Paterson]],<ref name="Best">[http://www.timesonline.co.uk/tol/sport/article1117695.ece Caught in Time: George Best joins Hibs, 1979], ''[[The Times]]'', 9 March 2003.</ref> Paterson also played for the [[Edinburgh]] club until he was signed by [[Rangers F.C.|Rangers]] for [[British pound sterling|£]]200,000 in August 1982. He later played for [[Motherwell F.C.|Motherwell]], [[Kilmarnock F.C.|Kilmarnock]] and [[Hamilton Academical F.C.|Hamilton Academical]], before ending his playing career at junior side [[Glenafton Athletic F.C.|Glenafton]].

Paterson captained Rangers in the [[1984 Scottish League Cup Final (October)|1984/85 Scottish League Cup final]] and won the [[1990&ndash;91 Scottish Cup]] with Motherwell. He has worked in the Scottish sports media since his retirement, initially with [[Radio Forth]],<ref name="Best"/> before joining the BBC.

'''Jean-Yves Girard''' ({{IPA-fr|ʒiʁaʁ|lang}}; born 1947) is a French [[logician]] working in [[proof theory]]. He is the research director ([[emeritus]]) at the mathematical institute of [[Faculte des Sciences de Luminy|Luminy]].

== Biography ==

Jean-Yves Girard is an alumnus of the [[École normale supérieure de Saint-Cloud]].

He made a name for himself in the 1970s with his proof of [[strong normalization]] in a system of [[second-order logic]] called [[System F]]. This result gave a new proof of [[Takeuti's conjecture]], which was proven a few years earlier by [[William W. Tait]], Moto-o Takahashi and [[Dag Prawitz]]. For this purpose, he introduced the notion of "reducibility candidate" ("candidat de réducibilité").

'''Oswald von Nell-Breuning''' [[Society of Jesus|SJ]] (March 8, 1890 &ndash; August 21, 1991) was a [[Roman Catholic]] theologian and [[sociology|sociologist]].

Born in [[Trier]], [[Germany]] into an aristocratic family, Nell-Breuning was ordained in 1921 and appointed Professor of Ethics at the [[Goethe University Frankfurt|University of Frankfurt am Main]] in 1928. He was instrumental in the drafting of [[Pope Pius XI]]'s social [[encyclical]] ''[[Quadragesimo anno]]'' (1931), which &ndash; like the earlier ''[[Rerum novarum]]'' (1891), after which it was named &ndash; dealt with the "Social Question" and developed the principle of [[subsidiarity (Catholicism)|subsidiarity]]. Nell-Breuning was not allowed to publish from 1936 to the end of [[Nazi Germany]] in 1945. He died in [[Frankfurt am Main]].

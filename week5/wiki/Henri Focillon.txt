'''Henri Focillon''' (7 September 1881 – 3 March 1943) was a [[France|French]] art historian.

Director of the Musée des Beaux-Arts in Lyon. Professor of [[Art History]] at the University of [[Lyon]], at the Ecole des Beaux-Arts in Lyon, at the [[University of Paris|Sorbonne]], at the [[Collège de France]] and then in the United States, where he went into exile and taught at Yale University.  [[Poet]], printmaker, and a [[teacher]] without equal, Henri Focillon formed generations of art historians including [[George Kubler]].  He remains best known for his works on medieval art, most of which were translated into English.

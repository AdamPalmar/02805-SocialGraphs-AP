'''Leopold Löwenheim''' (26 June 1878 in [[Krefeld]] – 5 May 1957 in [[Berlin]]) was a [[Germany|German]] [[mathematician]], known for his work in [[mathematical logic]]. The [[Nazism|Nazi]] regime forced him to retire because under the [[Nuremberg Laws]] he was considered only three quarters [[Aryan]]. In 1943 much of his work was destroyed during a [[bombing]] raid on Berlin. Nevertheless, he survived the [[Second World War]], after which he resumed teaching [[mathematics]].<ref>{{MacTutor Biography|id=Lowenheim}}</ref>

Löwenheim (1915) gave the first proof of what is now known as the [[Löwenheim–Skolem theorem]], often considered the starting point for [[model theory]].

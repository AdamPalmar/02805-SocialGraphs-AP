'''Norman Daniels''', born in 1942, is an [[American philosopher|American political philosopher]] and [[philosopher of science]], [[political theorist]], [[ethicist]], and [[bioethics|bioethicist]] at [[Harvard University]] and the [[Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health]].<ref>[http://www.hsph.harvard.edu/faculty/norman-daniels/ HSPH faculty profile]</ref>  Before his career at Harvard, Daniels had built his career as a [[medical ethics|medical ethicist]] at [[Tufts University]] in [[Medford, Massachusetts]], and at [[Tufts University School of Medicine]], also in [[Boston]].

==Teaching positions==
Until his retirement at the end of June 2017, Daniels is Mary B. Saltonstall Professor of Population Ethics and Professor of Ethics and Population Health<ref>[http://www.hsph.harvard.edu/faculty/norman-daniels/ Norman Daniels, Faculty Directory, Department of Global Health and Population]</ref> in the Department of Global Health and Population at the [[Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health]] in Boston.

Previously, and for 33 years, he had taught political philosophy at [[Tufts University]] in [[Medford, Massachusetts]].<ref>[http://www.hsph.harvard.edu/benchmark/ndaniels/pdf/justice_health.pdf Justice, Health, and Healthcare, an article on political and social justice vis a vis access to healthcare, while Norman Daniels taught at Tufts]</ref><ref name=autogenerated2>[http://www.eqv.name/biographies/Bios_63-64.htm#_Toc128730656 Bios_63-64 htm<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>  At [[Tufts University]], he had been Goldthwaite Professor and chair of the philosophy department, and at [[Tufts University School of Medicine]], he was professor of [[medical ethics]] (1969–2002).<ref>[http://ethics.harvard.edu/people/current-role/faculty-associates Faculty Associates of the Edward J. Safra Center for Ethics at Harvard, noting some of the topical areas which Dr. Daniels researches]</ref>

==Education==

==Personal life==
Daniels is married to neuro-psychologist Anne Lacy Daniels (Ed.D.).<ref>[http://healthprovidersdata.com/hipaa/codes/NPI-1104030949-dr-anne-lacy-daniels-anne-daniels-edd Health Providers Data for Anne Lacy Daniels, EdD]</ref>  They have one son, Noah M. Daniels, formerly a postdoctoral research associate at [[MIT]], is now Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science and Statistics of The [[University of Rhode Island]].<ref>[http://people.csail.mit.edu/ndaniels/ MIT CSAIL profile for Noah M. Daniels, PhD]</ref>

With [[Jared Israel]], Daniels co-chaired the Harvard chapter of the [[Students for a Democratic Society]] in 1969.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://select.nytimes.com/gst/abstract.html?res=F50F14FA395E1B7493C0A9178ED85F4D8685F9|title=169 Fined in Harvard Sit-In; 2 Cleared at Cambridge|accessdate=February 9, 2009|author=Robert M. Smith|date=May 2, 1969|publisher=[[The New York Times]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://select.nytimes.com/gst/abstract.html?res=F10D14FD3B5B1A7B93CBA9178ED85F438685F9|title=70 Youths Ejected In Protest on Draft At House Building|accessdate=February 9, 2009|date=May 9, 1967|publisher=[[The New York Times]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-8115756.html|title=Harvard, Ex-Radicals Remember Many Talk of Feelings 20 Years After Protest|accessdate=February 9, 2009|author=Alexander Reid|date=April 9, 1989|publisher=[[The Boston Globe]]}}</ref>

In a public letter to his fraternity brothers at Wesleyan, Daniels wrote: "At Harvard, I ended up co-chair of SDS and gave the speech on the steps of University Hall April 9, 1969, that began the take-over of that administration building and thus led to the Harvard Strike. I would have been fired as a teaching fellow, so I followed my advisors advice and quit that position to take a part-time job at Tufts, teaching philosophy of science and political philosophy. I stayed 33 years."<ref name=autogenerated2 />

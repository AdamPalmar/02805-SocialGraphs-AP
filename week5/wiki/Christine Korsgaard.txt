'''Christine Marion Korsgaard''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|k|ɔr|z|g|ɑː|r|d}}; born April 9, 1952) is an [[United States|American]] [[philosopher]] and academic whose main scholarly interests are in [[Ethics|moral philosophy]] and its history; the relation of issues in moral philosophy to issues in [[metaphysics]], the [[philosophy of mind]], and the theory of [[personal identity]]; the theory of [[personal relationship]]s; and in [[Norm (sociology)|normativity]] in general. She has taught at [[Yale University|Yale]], the [[University of California at Santa Barbara]], and the [[University of Chicago]]; since 1991 she has been a professor at [[Harvard University]], where she is now Arthur Kingsley Porter Professor of Philosophy.  She has been described as "one of today's leading moral philosophers"<ref>https://ndpr.nd.edu/news/24047-the-constitution-of-agency-essays-on-practical-reason-and-moral-psychology/</ref> because of her work in defense of [[Kantian ethics|Kantian views in moral theory]].

==Biography==
Korsgaard first attended Eastern Illinois University for two years and transferred to receive a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] from the [[University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign|University of Illinois]] and a [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D]] from Harvard, where she was a student of [[John Rawls]]. She received an LHD [[Doctor of Humane Letters]] from the [[University of Illinois]] in 2004.<ref>http://commencement.illinois.edu/ceremonies/honors_awards.html</ref> She is a 1970 alumna of [[Homewood-Flossmoor High School]] in Flossmoor, Ill.

In 1996 Korsgaard published a book entitled ''The Sources of Normativity'', which was the revised version of her [[Tanner Lectures on Human Values]], and also a collection of her past papers on [[Immanuel Kant|Kant]]'s moral philosophy and Kantian approaches to contemporary moral philosophy: ''Creating the Kingdom of Ends''. In 2002, she was the first woman to give the [[John Locke Lectures]] at the [[University of Oxford]],<ref>http://www.philosophy.ox.ac.uk/lectures/john_locke_lectures/past_lectures</ref> which turned into her most recent book, ''Self-Constitution: Agency, Identity, and Integrity''.

She was elected a Fellow of the [[American Academy of Arts & Sciences]] in 2001<ref>http://news.harvard.edu/gazette/2001/05.24/07-academy.html</ref> and a Corresponding Fellow of the [[British Academy]] in 2015.<ref>http://www.britac.ac.uk/fellowship/elections/index.cfm?year=2015</ref>

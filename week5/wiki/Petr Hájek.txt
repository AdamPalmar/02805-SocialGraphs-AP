'''Petr Hájek''' ({{IPA-cs|ˈpɛtr̩ ˈɦaːjɛk}}; 6 February 1940 – 26 December 2016) was a [[Czechs|Czech]] [[scientist]] in the area of [[mathematical logic]]<ref>{{cite news | url = http://www.radio.cz/cz/clanek/91701 | work = [[Český rozhlas]] | language = Czech | title = Náš host: Matematik s hudebními sklony | date = 26 May 2007 | first = Zdeněk | last = Vališ}}</ref> and a professor of [[mathematics]]. Born in [[Prague]], he worked at the Institute of [[Computer Science]] at the [[Academy of Sciences of the Czech Republic]] and as a lecturer at the Faculty of Mathematics and Physics at the [[Charles University in Prague]] and at the Faculty of Nuclear Sciences and Physical Engineering of the [[Czech Technical University in Prague]]. 

==Academics==
Petr Hájek studied at the Faculty of Mathematics and Physics of the [[Charles University in Prague]]<ref name=CV>http://www.cs.cas.cz/hajek/</ref> Influenced by [[Petr Vopěnka]], he specialized in [[set theory]], [[arithmetic]], later also in [[logic]] and [[artificial intelligence]].<ref name=VIT>http://vit.ad.umel.feec.vutbr.cz/portalZS/ContentPage.aspx?PageID=b97b0db3-8760-44b8-8b8c-2794d505e0d8</ref> He contributed to establishing the mathematical fundamentals of [[fuzzy logic]].<ref name=VIT/> Following the [[Velvet Revolution]] he was appointed a [[senior lecturer]] (1993), and a professor (1997).<ref name=CV/> From 1992 to 2000 he held the position of chairman of the Institute of Computer Science at the Academy of Sciences of the Czech Republic.<ref name=CV/> From 1996 to 2003 he was also president of the [[Kurt Gödel Society]].<ref>{{cite web | url = http://kgs.logic.at/index.php?id=42 | title = Our Presidents | work = [[Kurt Gödel Society]]}}</ref> 

Later he graduated from [[Academy of Performing Arts in Prague]], where he studied [[Pipe organ]] under Jiří Reinberger to become an organ player in a church.<ref>http://www.radio.cz/cz/rubrika/kaleidoskop/nas-host-matematik-s-hudebnimi-sklony</ref>

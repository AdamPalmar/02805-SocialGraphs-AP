'''Glenn E. McGee''' is a [[professor]] of [[management]] at the [[University of New Haven]], who focuses on [[health policy]].<ref>{{citation|title=University of New Haven : McGee, Glenn|url=http://www.newhaven.edu/Faculty-Staff-Profiles/787295/|accessdate=2014-08-11}}</ref> He has been noted for his work on [[reproductive technology]] and [[genetics]] and for advancing a theory of pragmatic bioethics, as well as the role of ethicists in society and in local and state settings in particular.<ref>{{citation|first=Glenn|last=McGee|title=Parenting in an Era of Genetics|url=https://www.questia.com/googleScholar.qst;jsessionid=Ly0LvgGZyYvzhD0LzchjrlhlCPQzq1lfYncLvvbJs1CtzRpDycxp!-1667364753?docId=5002234765|journal=Hastings Center Report|volume=27|year=1997|accessdate=2008-07-07}}</ref><ref name="born">{{citation|first=Roxanne|last=Patel|title=The Born Identity: Penn's Glenn McGee, one of the best-known bioethicists in the world, tries to make sense of the most controversial issues of our day. ==Life and career==
McGee was raised by adoptive parents on the faculty of [[Baylor University]] in [[Waco, Texas]]. He earned a master's degree and Ph.D. in [[philosophy]] from [[Vanderbilt University]] and completed a post-doctoral fellowship in the [[National Human Genome Research Institute|Human Genome Project]].<ref name="born"/>

From 1995 to 2005, McGee was an assistant professor and associate director for education at the [[University of Pennsylvania]] Center for Bioethics, where he held joint appointments in philosophy, history and sociology of science, cellular and molecular engineering, and was a Fellow of the [[Leonard Davis Institute of Health Economics]]. In 1999, he founded and became the first [[editor-in-chief]] of ''[[The American Journal of Bioethics]]''. In 2005, he moved to [[Union University (New York)|Union University]] in [[Albany, New York]], as the John Balint, M.D. Professor of Medical Ethics and became director of the Alden March Bioethics Institute at [[Albany Medical College]] which had been founded in 1993 as the Center for Medical Ethics Education and Research by Balint. Three years later, after a legal case arising from the University's attempts to demote him as director and remove his endowed chair, he left the university.<ref>{{citation|first=Cathleen|last=Crowley|title=Bioethics expert in dispute with Albany Med.: Glenn McGee files complaint in court saying college is not honoring severance package|url=http://www.timesunion.com/AspStories/story.asp?storyID=697584|journal=Albany Times Union|date=June 20, 2008|accessdate=2008-06-24}}</ref><ref>{{citation|last=Borrell|first=Brendan|journal=Scientific American|date=June 16, 2008|title=An Unethical Ethicist? Was questionable behavior behind the abrupt departure of Alden March Bioethics Institute's chief?|url=http://www.sciam.com/article.cfm?id=glenn-mcgee}}</ref><ref>{{citation|last=Borrell|first=Brendan|journal=Scientific American|date=July 7, 2008|title=Alden March Bioethics Institute Picks Up the Pieces: Embattled bioethicist sues Albany Medical College over severance package|url=http://www.sciam.com/article.cfm?id=bioethics-institute-picks}}</ref> In 2009 he was appointed to the John B. Francis chair in bioethics at the Center for Practical Bioethics in Kansas City, succeeding [[John D. Lantos]], inaugural holder of the chair.<ref>{{citation|title=New home for McGee|url=http://www.timesunion.com/AspStories/story.asp?storyID=877474&category=BUSINESS|date=December 13, 2009|accessdate=2010-01-02|publisher=[[Albany Times-Union]]}}</ref> He is presently a professor of management on the faculty of the Department of [[Management]] in the [[University of New Haven]] [[business school]] in 2014, where he teaches  [[public health]] and [[health law]].<ref>{{citation|title=University of New Haven : McGee, Glenn : Academic Credentials Subtab|url=http://www.newhaven.edu/Faculty-Staff-Profiles/787295/|accessdate=2014-08-11}}</ref>

== Publications ==
McGee has authored many scholarly articles, essays, reviews, three books, and edited a number of books both personally and as senior editor of [The MIT Press] Basic Bioethics book series, which he founded with [[Arthur Caplan]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mitpress.mit.edu/catalog/browse/browse.asp?btype=6&serid=23 |title=Basic Bioethics |publisher=The MIT Press |date= |accessdate=2012-02-17}}</ref> His proposal for a California cloning policy was reprinted in ''Great American Speeches'', and a number of his articles have been reprinted in textbooks in bioethics, medical and other scientific fields. From 2005 to 2007, he wrote a monthly column for ''[[The Scientist (magazine)|The Scientist]]'', and during the same time a column for the ''[[Albany Times-Union]]''. Prior to that, he co-wrote a column on bioethics for MSNBC.com.<ref>{{cite web|last=McGee |first=Glenn |url=http://bioethics.net/articles.php?viewCat=2&page=8 |title=Article Topics: Bioethics on MSNBC |publisher=bioethics.net |date= |accessdate=2012-02-17}}</ref>

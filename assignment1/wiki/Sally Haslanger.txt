'''Sally Haslanger''' is the Ford Professor of [[Philosophy]] in the Department of Linguistics and Philosophy at the [[Massachusetts Institute of Technology]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mit.edu/~philos/haslanger.html|title=MIT philosophy faculty: Sally Haslanger|author=|date=|website=www.MIT.edu|access-date=June 11, 2017}}</ref> and holds the 2015 Spinoza Chair of Philosophy at the [[University of Amsterdam]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uva.nl/over-de-uva/organisatie/faculteiten/content/faculteit-der-geesteswetenschappen/agenda/agenda/agenda/content/folder-13/lectures/2015/04/spinoza-lecture-ideology-and-materiality.html?page=3&pageSize=20|title=Spinoza Lecture: Ideology and Materiality – Faculteit der Geesteswetenschappen – Universiteit van Amsterdam|author=Universiteit van Amsterdam|work=uva.nl|access-date=}}</ref> Since 2009, she has also served as director of the Women's and Gender Studies program.<ref>[http://www.mit.edu/~shaslang/HaslangerCV.pdf]</ref>

==Biography==
Having graduated from [[Reed College]] in 1977, Haslanger earned her Ph.D. in 1985 from the [[University of California, Berkeley]]. She has taught at [[Princeton University]], [[University of Pennsylvania]] and [[University of Michigan, Ann Arbor]].<ref>[http://web.mac.com/shaslang/Sally_Haslanger/Curriculum_Vitae.html]</ref>

Haslanger was selected as the 2011 [[Carus Lecturer]] by the [[American Philosophical Association]].<ref name="2010awards">{{cite web|url=http://shass.mit.edu/news/news-2010-haslanger-receives-two-major-philosophy-awards|title=MIT SHASS: News 2010 – Haslanger receives two major philosophy awards|work=MIT.edu|access-date=}}</ref> The Society for Women in Philosophy named her a 2010 Distinguished Woman Philosopher, citing her as one of the "best analytic feminists" in the United States.<ref name="2010awards" /> Haslanger was the president of the Eastern Division of the American Philosophical Association and was elected to the [[American Academy of Arts & Sciences]] in 2015.<ref name=mitnews>{{cite web|title=Eight faculty members elected to the American Academy of Arts and Sciences|url=http://newsoffice.mit.edu/2015/eight-faculty-members-elected-american-academy-arts-and-sciences-0422|website=MIT News|publisher=MIT|access-date=May 27, 2015}}</ref>

Haslanger co-edits the ''Symposia on Gender, Race and Philosophy'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sgrp.typepad.com/|title=Gender, Race and Philosophy: The Blog|author=|date=|website=Gender, Race and Philosophy: The Blog|access-date=June 11, 2017}}</ref> an online publication for recent philosophical work on gender and race.<ref>[http://web.mac.com/shaslang/Sally_Haslanger/Professional_Activities.html]</ref>

She is married to fellow MIT philosopher [[Stephen Yablo]].<ref name="aboutme" />

She has a son, Isaac, who attends Howard University in Washington, D.C. and a daughter, Zina, who attends college in Massachusetts.{{citation needed|date=May 2015}}

==Work==
Haslanger has published in [[metaphysics]], [[feminist metaphysics]], [[epistemology]], [[feminist theory]], [[ancient philosophy]], and social and [[political philosophy]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mit.edu/~philos/haslanger.html|title=MIT philosophy faculty: Sally Haslanger|work=mit.edu}}</ref> Much of her work has focused on persistence and endurance through change; objectivity and objectification; and [[Catharine MacKinnon]]'s theory of gender. She has done groundbreaking work on the social construction of categories often considered to be natural kinds, particularly race and gender.<ref name="aboutme">[http://web.mac.com/shaslang/Sally_Haslanger/About_Me.html]</ref> A collection of her major papers on these topics appeared as ''Resisting Reality: Social Construction and Social Critique'' (Oxford University Press, 2012) which won the [[Joseph B. Gittler Award]] of the American Philosophical Association in 2014. This prize is given for an outstanding scholarly contribution in the field of the philosophy of one or more of the social sciences. The range of the social sciences is construed broadly so as to include anthropology, economics, education, government, history, psychology, sociology, and any other field that is normally located within the social science division in contemporary colleges and universities.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.apaonline.org/?gittler|title=Joseph B. Gittler Award – The American Philosophical Association|work=APAOnline.org|access-date=}}</ref>

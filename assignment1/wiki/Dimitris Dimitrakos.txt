'''Dimitris Dimitrakos''' ({{lang-el|Δημήτρης Δημητράκος}}; born 1936) is a [[Greece|Greek]] [[philosopher]], currently Professor [[Emeritus]] of [[Political Philosophy]] in the [[Philosophy of Science]] Department of the [[University of Athens]]. He originally studied [[economics]] at the [[London School of Economics]]), taking part in seminars given there by [[Karl Popper]], before obtaining a [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D.]] in Political Philosophy at the [[University of Paris]] with a [[thesis]] on [[Antonio Gramsci]]. Since then he has taught at the Universities of Paris, [[University of Reims Champagne-Ardenne|Reims]], [[University of Thessaly|Thessaly]] and Athens and as a visiting professor at the LSE.

Dimitrakos' work covers many areas of [[epistemology]], [[political philosophy]] and [[social theory]] which include [[Karl Popper#Philosophy of science|Karl Popper's philosophy of science]] and society, analytical aspects of [[Marxism]], the role of [[rationalism]] and [[liberalism]] in social and [[political theory]] as well as [[individual rights]] and [[Democracy|democratic theory]].

He has also written extensively on [[civil society]], [[nationalism]] and current political problems. His research interests focus on [[economic theory]], liberalism and the [[Philosophy of social science#Epistemology|epistemology of social sciences]].

In July 2002, he was a speaker at the [[Karl Popper]] centenary conference held in [[Vienna]].<ref>[http://pub19.bravenet.com/forum/static/show.php?usernum=1573050381&frmid=627&msgid=19426 Dimitrakos' paper "The Weapon of Criticism".]</ref>

He is currently President of the "Open Society",<ref>[http://facebook.com/opensocietyclub Open Society association]</ref> an association for the promotion of principles of [[democracy]], [[liberalism]], the [[Rechtsstaat|rule of law]], [[human rights]] and [[civil society]] in Greece.

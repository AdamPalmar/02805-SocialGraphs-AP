'''Alasdair Ian Fenton Urquhart''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|æ|l|ə|s|d|ər|_|ˈ|ɜr|k|ər|t}}; born 20 December 1945) is an [[emeritus]] [[Professor]] of [[Philosophy]] at the [[University of Toronto]]. He has made contributions to the field of [[logic]], especially [[non-classical logic]].  One of his ideas is proving the [[decidability (logic)|undecidability]] of the [[relevance logic]] '''R'''. He also published papers in [[theoretical computer science]] venues, mostly on [[mathematical logic]] topics of [[logic in computer science|relevance to computer science]].

A native of Scotland,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.whockey.com/work/dcs/dcs/people/faculty/urquhart.html|title="Alasdair Urquhart was born in Scotland in 1945"|publisher=Department of Computer Science - University of Toronto|accessdate=2 April 2010}}</ref> Urquhart received his [[MA (Scotland)|MA]] in Philosophy from the [[University of Edinburgh]] in 1967, and his [[Ph.D.]] from the [[University of Pittsburgh]] in 1973 under the supervision of [[Alan Ross Anderson]] and [[Nuel Belnap]].

== Selected publications ==
* "The Undecidability of Entailment and Relevant Implication."  ''[[Journal of Symbolic Logic]]'' 49(4): 1059-1073 (1984).
* with [[Stephen A. Cook]], "Functional Interpretations of Feasibly constructive Arithmetic", ''[[Annals of Pure and Applied Logic]]'', 1993; preliminary version at [[STOC]]'89
* "The Complexity of Decision Procedures in Relevance Logic II", J. Symbolic Logic, Volume 64, Issue 4 (1999), 1774-1802.

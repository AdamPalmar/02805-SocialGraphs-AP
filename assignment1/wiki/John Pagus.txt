'''John Pagus'''<ref>John Le Page, Johannes Pagus, Jean Le Page, Jean Lepage.</ref> (active first half of the 13th century) was a [[scholastic philosopher]] at the [[University of Paris]], generally considered the first [[logician]] writing at the Arts faculty at Paris.<ref>Bertil Malmberg, ''Histoire de la Linguistique'', Paris: Presses universitaires de France, 1991, p. 127.</ref>

==Life==
He is thought to have been a [[Master of Arts]] in the 1220s and to have taught [[Peter of Spain (author)|Peter of Spain]].<ref>[http://plato.stanford.edu/entries/peter-spain/ ''Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy'', article ''Peter of Spain'']</ref> At that time he was writing on [[syncategorematic]] terms.<ref>Sten Ebbesen, Russell L. Friedman (editors), ''Medieval Analyses in Language and Cognition'' (1999), p. 36.</ref><ref>Parts published in [[Henk Braakhuis|H. A. G. Braakhuis]], ''De 13de Eeuwse Tractaten over Syncategorematische Termen''. Vol. I, Ph. Diss., Leiden University, 1979.</ref>

'''Leo Anthony Harrington''' (born May 17, 1946) is a professor of mathematics at the [[University of California, Berkeley]] who works in 
[[recursion theory]], [[model theory]], and [[set theory]].

* Harrington and [[Jeff Paris]] proved the [[Paris–Harrington theorem]].<ref name=Paris>{{Citation

 | last = Paris
 | first2 = L.
 | last2 = Harrington

 | title = Handbook of Mathematical Logic
 | pages = 1133–1142
 | publisher = North-Holland
}}</ref>

* Harrington showed that if the [[Determinacy|Axiom of Determinacy]] holds for all [[analytic set]]s then [[Zero sharp#Other sharps|x<sup>#</sup>]] exists for all reals x.<ref name=Harrington1978>{{citation

 | jstor=2273508
}}</ref>

* Harrington and [[Saharon Shelah]] showed that the first-order theory of the partially ordered set of recursively enumerable [[Turing degrees]] is undecidable.<ref name=Harrington1982>{{citation

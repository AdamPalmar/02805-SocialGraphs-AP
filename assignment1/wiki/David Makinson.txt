'''David Clement Makinson''', D.Phil, (born 27 August 1941), is an [[Australian]] mathematical [[logician]] living in [[London]], [[England]].

== Career ==
Makinson began his studies at [[University of Sydney|Sydney University]] in 1958 and was an associate of the [[Libertarianism|Libertarian]] Society and [[Sydney Push]]. He is a Visiting Professor in the [[London School of Economics]], [[University of London]], and an associate member of the [[Centre de Recherche en Epistémologie Appliquée]] (CREA), [[École Polytechnique]], [[Paris]]. He has held professorial rank positions in [[King's College London]], University of London and in the [[American University of Beirut]], [[Lebanon]]. From 1980 till 2001 he worked for [[UNESCO]], [[Paris]].

== Contribution ==
David Makinson is highly regarded for his work on [[belief revision]], [[Inference#Inference and uncertainty|uncertain reasoning]], and [[modal logic]]. While studying in [[Oxford University]] ([[Worcester College]]) for his [[Doctor of Philosophy|D.Phil]] under the supervision of [[Michael Dummett]], he identified the [[preface paradox]]. In belief revision he created the [[Belief revision#The AGM postulates|AGM account of theory change]] with [[Carlos Alchourrón]] and [[Peter Gärdenfors]]. In modal logic and other [[non-classical logic]]s, he showed how to establish [[completeness (logic)|completeness]] results by adapting the method of [[maximal consistent set]]. In 1969 Makinson discovered the first simple and natural [[propositional logic]] lacking the [[Kripke semantics#Finite model property|finite model property]]. Together with [[Leon van der Torre]] he developed [[input/output logic]].

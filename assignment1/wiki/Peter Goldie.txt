'''Peter Goldie''' (5 November 1946 – 22 October 2011) was a British academic philosopher with interests in ethics and aesthetics. He was the Samuel Hall Chair in Philosophy and Head of the [http://www.socialsciences.manchester.ac.uk/philosophy/ Philosophy Discipline Area] of the [http://www.socialsciences.man.ac.uk/school/ School of Social Sciences] at [[University of Manchester]]. He was educated at [[Felsted School|Felsted]].

After a 25-year career in business in the City of London, culminating as chief executive at the ill-fated [[British and Commonwealth Holdings|British & Commonwealth]], Goldie turned to Philosophy, in 1990.<ref>[http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/obituaries/culture-obituaries/books-obituaries/8853815/Professor-Peter-Goldie.html Obituary in the Telegraph]</ref> He studied at [[University College London]] for a [[Bachelor of Arts|BA]] degree and at [[Balliol College, Oxford]] for a [[Bachelor of Philosophy|BPhil]] followed by a [[Doctor of Philosophy|DPhil]], supervised by [[Bernard Williams]], on emotion, mood and character. Following this, he was a lecturer at [[Magdalen College, Oxford]], for two years and [[King's College London]] before becoming a professor at Manchester in 2005.

Peter Goldie died on 22 October 2011.<ref>[http://leiterreports.typepad.com/blog/2011/10/in-memoriam-peter-goldie-1946-2011.html In Memoriam: Peter Goldie (1946-2011)]</ref>

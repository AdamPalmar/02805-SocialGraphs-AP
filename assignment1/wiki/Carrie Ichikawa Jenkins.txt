'''Carrie Ichikawa Jenkins''' holds a [[Canada Research Chair]]<ref name=autogenerated1>{{cite web|url=http://www.chairs-chaires.gc.ca/chairholders-titulaires/profile-eng.aspx?profileId=2870|title=Canada Research Chair – Profile|work=chairs-chaires.gc.ca}}</ref> and is Professor of Philosophy at the [[University of British Columbia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://philosophy.ubc.ca/persons/carrie-jenkins/|title=Carrie Jenkins – Department of Philosophy|work=ubc.ca}}</ref> She is also a Professor at the Northern Institute of Philosophy, [[University of Aberdeen]].<ref>[http://www.abdn.ac.uk/nip/page?id=73 Northern Institute of Philosophy] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140326183100/http://www.abdn.ac.uk/nip/page?id=73 |date=March 26, 2014 }}</ref> Her primary research areas are [[epistemology]], [[metaphysics]], [[philosophy of logic]], [[philosophy of language]], and [[philosophy of mathematics]].<ref name=autogenerated2>http://dl.dropboxusercontent.com/u/2480139/CV.pdf</ref> She is one of the principal editors of the journal ''[[Thought: A Journal of Philosophy|Thought]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/10.1002/(ISSN)2161-2234|title=Thought: A Journal of Philosophy – Wiley Online Library|work=wiley.com}}</ref>

==Education and career==
Jenkins holds BA, MPhil and PhD degrees in philosophy from [[Trinity College, Cambridge]].<ref name=autogenerated2 />

She has held teaching and research positions at the [[University of St Andrews]], the [[Australian National University]], the [[University of Michigan]] and the [[University of Nottingham]].<ref name=autogenerated2 /> She was head of the department of philosophy at the University of Nottingham from 2010 to 2011.<ref name=autogenerated2 />

Jenkins was awarded a [[Canada Research Chair]] by the Canadian Government in 2011.<ref name=autogenerated1 />

Jenkins is also a member of the philosophy-themed musical group ''The 21st Century Monads''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://the21stcenturymonads.net/|title=The 21st Century Monads|work=the21stcenturymonads.net}}</ref>

Jenkins became involved in a dispute with [[Brian Leiter]], a professor at the [[University of Chicago]] and then editor of the [[Philosophical Gourmet Report]]. His e-mailed remarks about Jenkins led to hundreds of philosophers refusing to provide data for the report, and ultimately led to his stepping down as its editor.<ref name=chron1>{{cite web|last1=Schmidt|first1=Peter|title=The Man Who Ranks Philosophy Departments Now Rankles Them, Too|url=http://chronicle.com/article/The-Man-Who-Ranks-Philosophy/149007/|website=Chronicle.com|publisher=The Chronicle of Higher Education|accessdate=September 30, 2014|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140926193400/http://chronicle.com/article/The-Man-Who-Ranks-Philosophy/149007/|archivedate=September 26, 2014|deadurl=no|date=September 26, 2014}}</ref><ref name="chronicle.com1">{{cite web|url=http://chronicle.com/blogs/ticker/controversial-philosopher-will-step-down-as-editor-of-influential-rankings/87797|title=Controversial Philosopher Will Step Down as Editor of Influential Rankings|work=[[The Chronicle of Higher Education]] |date=October 10, 2014|author=Andy Thomason}}</ref>

==Research areas==
Jenkins is the author of ''Grounding Concepts: An Empirical Basis for Arithmetical Knowledge'', published in 2008 by Oxford University Press.

''Grounding Concepts'' has been described as an ‘important book’,<ref>J.R. Brown, Platonism, Naturalism, and Mathematical Knowledge, Routledge Studies in the Philosophy of Science, 2011, {{ISBN|978-0415872669}}, p. 67</ref> ‘an original treatment of the epistemology of arithmetic that is clearly articulated and carefully argued’,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.unl.edu/philosophy/people/faculty/casullo/Jenkins.pdf |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2014-04-21 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140423054037/http://www.unl.edu/philosophy/people/faculty/casullo/Jenkins.pdf |archivedate=2014-04-23 |df= }}</ref> and a 'key work' in its field.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://philpapers.org/browse/apriority-in-mathematics|title=Apriority in Mathematics|work=philpapers.org}}</ref> Reviewers have said that ‘at each step Jenkins offers new ideas, or interesting twists of the traditional ones, or exciting polemics and criticism’,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.academia.edu/1850741/On_C._Jenkins_Grounding_Concepts_|title=On C. Jenkins_Grounding Concepts:|author=Nenad G Miscevic|work=academia.edu}}</ref> and that "Grounding Concepts" ‘adds a genuinely new option to the philosophical landscape.’<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ndpr.nd.edu/news/24346/?id=19668|title=Grounding Concepts: An Empirical Basis for Arithmetical Knowledge|author=ENR // AgencyND // University of Notre Dame|work=nd.edu}}</ref>

Jenkins has also published over thirty articles in philosophical journals and edited volumes.<ref name=autogenerated2 /> In 2013, she was a keynote speaker at the annual conference of the Western Canadian Philosophical Association.<ref name=autogenerated2 />

Jenkins’s work on the philosophy of flirting has been described as ‘ground-breaking’.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/book/10.1002/9781444324549|title=Dating – Philosophy for Everyone: Flirting with Big Ideas|work=wiley.com}}</ref>  She is currently leading a research project on the metaphysics of romantic love<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themetaphysicsoflove.com/|title=The Metaphysics of Love|work=The Metaphysics of Love}}</ref> and is well-known as a defender of polyamory.<ref>http://www.chronicle.com/article/I-Have-Multiple-Loves-/239077</ref>

'''L. T. F. Gamut''' was a collective [[pseudonym]] for the [[Netherlands|Dutch]] [[logic]]ians [[Johan van Benthem (logician)|Johan van Benthem]], [[Jeroen Groenendijk]], [[Dick de Jongh]], [[Martin Stokhof]] and [[Henk Verkuyl]].<ref>Preface to ''Logic, Language and Meaning'', by L. T. F. Gamut, University of Chicago Press, 1991.</ref>

Gamut stands for the Dutch universities of [[University of Groningen|Groningen]] ('''G'''), [[University of Amsterdam|Amsterdam]] ('''am'''), and [[Utrecht University|Utrecht]] ('''ut'''), then the affiliations of the authors.<ref>{{jstor|2275619}}</ref> The initials L. T. F. stand for the discussed topics, respectively, Logic (Dutch: '''''L'''ogica''), Language (Dutch: '''''T'''aal'') and Philosophy (Dutch: '''''F'''ilosofie'').

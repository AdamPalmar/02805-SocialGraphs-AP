'''Heiric of Auxerre''' <ref>Heiricus Autissiodorensis or Altissiodorensis, Eric of Auxerre.</ref> (841â€“876) was a French [[Benedictine]] theologian and writer.

He was an [[Oblate (religion)|oblate]] of  the [[monastery]] of [[St. Germanus of Auxerre]] from a young age. He studied with [[Servatus Lupus]] and [[Haymo of Auxerre]]. His own students included [[Remigius of Auxerre]] and [[Hucbald]].

His ''Miracula sancti Germani'' was a verse life of [[St. Germanus]]. Other works include his ''Collectaeum'',<ref>''... a florilegium consisting mainly of extracts from classical authors, particularly [[Valerius Maximus]]'', Rosamond McKitterick, ''The Frankish Kingdoms under the Carolingians'' (1983), p. 290.</ref> a [[homiliary]], and glosses on the ''[[Categoriae decem]]''.

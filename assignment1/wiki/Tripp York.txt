'''Fred "Tripp" York''' is a professor of religious studies, and a prolific Mennonite writer (B.A., [[Trevecca Nazarene University]]; M.T.S., [[Duke University]]; Ph.D., Garrett-Evangelical Theological Seminary).<ref name="Elon">{{cite web |url=http://www.elon.edu/e-web/academics/elon_college/religious_studies/fac.xhtml |title=Elon University - Department of Religious Studies |publisher= Elon.edu|accessdate=2009-01-11}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://trippyork.com/|title=Tripp York, PhD|website=Tripp York, PhD|language=en-US|access-date=2016-03-30}}</ref> His writings span a wide range of genres and subjects including: animals, [[martyrdom]], politics, violence, religious satire and comics. His most popular work is his satirical search for Satan in ''The Devil Wears Nada''.

He is the co-creator and co-editor of [http://www.peaceablekingdomseries.com/ The Peaceable Kingdom Series].

York belongs to the [[Mennonite]] tradition that has a 500-year history of [[Christian pacifism]].<ref>Justo L. Gonzalez,''The Story of Christianity, Vol. II'', (San Francisco: Harper, 1985).</ref> He has written extensively on the North American Christians' complicity with power and suggests a return to a more diasporic understanding of Christian practice.<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://mattlitton.com/2011/07/08/is-the-church-narrating-what-it-means-to-be-an-american-or-is-america-narrating-what-it-means-to-be-the-church-five-good-answers-with-dr-tripp-york-author-of-third-way-allegiance/ |title=Is the church narrating what it means to be an American or is America narrating what it means to be the church? Five Good Answers with Dr. Tripp York, Author of Third Way Allegiance |publisher=Matt Litton |date=2011-07-08 |accessdate=2014-03-12}}</ref> He emphasizes the lives of [[Christian anarchists]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.elon.edu/e-net/Note.aspx?id=937429 |title=Christian anarchists topic of book by Tripp York |publisher=Elon.edu |date= |accessdate=2014-03-12}}</ref> such as [[Dorothy Day]], and [[Daniel Berrigan|Daniel]] and [[Philip Berrigan]].

He teaches at [[Virginia Wesleyan College]]<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://www.vwc.edu/religious-studies/faculty-and-staff.php?person_id=0570661 |title=Virginia Wesleyan College - A small Liberal Arts College located in Norfolk, Virginia |publisher=Vwc.edu |date= |accessdate=2014-03-12}}</ref> in [[Norfolk, VA]].  He previously taught at [[Elon University]] and Western Kentucky University.

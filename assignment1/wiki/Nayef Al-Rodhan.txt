'''Nayef R. F. Al-Rodhan''' ({{lang-ar|نايف الروضان}}; born 1959) is a Saudi [[Philosophy|philosopher]], [[Neuroscience|neuroscientist]], [[Geostrategy|geostrategist]], and author. He is an Honorary Fellow of [[St. Antony’s College, Oxford|St. Antony’s College]] at Oxford University, Oxford, United Kingdom,<ref>[http://www.sant.ox.ac.uk/mec/fellows.html St. Antony’s College, University of Oxford]</ref> Senior Fellow and Centre Director of the Geopolitics and Global Futures Programme at the [[Geneva Centre for Security Policy]], Geneva, Switzerland.

==Biography==
Professor Nayef Al-Rodhan began his career as a [[Neurosurgery|neurosurgeon]] and [[Neuroscience|neuroscientist]]. As a medical student at [[Newcastle University]], he was mentored and influenced by the renowned [[neurologist]], [[John Walton, Baron Walton of Detchant|Lord John Walton of Detchant]]. He trained in [[neurosurgery]] and conducted [[neuroscience]] research at the [[Mayo Clinic]], [[Rochester, Minnesota]] in the United States. He became Chief Resident in [[neurosurgery]] and was influenced by Thoralf M. Sundt, David Piepgrass, and Patrick J Kelly at the [[Mayo Clinic]]. He obtained a Ph.D. in 1988 for his work on the ''Characterization of Opioid and Neurotensin Receptor Subtypes in the Brain with Respect to Antinociception''.<ref>[http://www.worldcat.org/title/characterization-of-opioid-and-neurotensin-receptor-subtypes-in-the-brain-with-respect-to-antinociception/oclc/170883051&referer=brief_results Characterization of opioid and neurotensin receptor subtypes in the brain with respect to antinociception, Thesis (Ph.D.) - Pharmacology - Mayo Graduate School of Medicine, 1988]</ref>

In 1993, on a fellowship from the [[Congress of Neurological Surgeons]], he joined the department of [[neurosurgery]] at the [[Yale University School of Medicine]] as a fellow in [[epilepsy]] surgery and molecular neuroscience under the direction of Dennis Spencer.<ref>[http://epilepsy.yale.edu/education/fellowships/neurosurgery/alumni.aspx Yale Epilepsy Program]</ref>

In 1994, Nayef Al-Rodhan became a fellow at the department of [[neurosurgery]] at the [[Massachusetts General Hospital]] at [[Harvard Medical School]], where he worked on the study of [[neuropeptide]]s, [[molecular genetics]], and [[neuronal]] [[Regeneration (biology)|regeneration]]. In 1995, he was appointed to the faculty of the [[Harvard Medical School]] and while at Harvard and [[Massachusetts General Hospital]], he founded the [[neurotechnology]] program with [[Nobel Prize]] winner [[James E. Muller]]. Working with Robert Martuza, Al-Rodhan also founded the Laboratories for Cellular Neurosurgery and Neurosurgical Technology at the department of [[neurosurgery]] of Massachusetts General Hospital, Harvard Medical School.<ref>[http://cnsr.mgh.harvard.edu/info.html Harvard Medical School]</ref><ref>[http://www.cimit.org/news/neurotechnology.html Center for Integration of Medicine & Innovative Technology, Neurotechnology Program]</ref>

===Neuroscience awards===
Nayef Al-Rodhan has received the following research awards: Sir James Spence Prize,<ref>[http://www.americanchronicle.com/articles/printFriendly/168564 American Chronicle]</ref> the Gibb Prize, the Farquhar-Murray Prize, the American Association of Neurological Surgeon Prize (twice), the Meninger Prize, the Annual Resident Prize of the Congress of Neurological Surgeons, the Young Investigator Prize of the [[American Association of Neurological Surgeons]], and the Annual Fellowship Prize of the [[Congress of Neurological Surgeons]].<ref>[http://www.gcsp.ch/About-Us/Staff/Faculty/Faculty-Members/Dr-Nayef-AL-RODHAN GCSP]</ref><ref>[http://www.pressclub.ch/menu/events/event_060710_1130.htm The Evian Group]</ref>

===Neuroscience and international relations===
Since 2002, Nayef Al-Rodhan has shifted his scholarly focus to the interplay between [[neuroscience]] and [[international relations]].<ref>[http://www.thenortherntimes.com.au/news/lifestyle/entertainment/books/neostatecraft-and-metageopolitics-lit-books-switzerland/1991055.aspx The Northern Times]</ref> Through several publications,<ref>[http://www.sustainablehistory.com/ Sustainable history]</ref> he has pioneered the application of neuroscience and the neuro-behavioural consequences of the [[neurochemical]] and cellular mechanisms that underpin [[emotion]]s, [[amorality]], [[Psychological egoism|egoism]]s, [[fear]], [[greed]], and [[:wikt:dominance|dominance]], into the analysis and conceptualization of trends in contemporary [[geopolitics]], [[global security]], [[national security]], [[Transculturation|transcultural]] security, and [[war]] and [[peace]].<ref>[http://www.americanchronicle.com/articles/view/194197 American Chronicle]</ref>

In 2006, Nayef Al-Rodhan joined the [[Geneva Center for Security Policy]] in Geneva, Switzerland, as a Senior Scholar in [[geostrategy]] and Director of the [[Geopolitics]] and Global Futures Programme.<ref>[http://www.gcsp.ch/About-Us/Staff/Faculty/Faculty-Members/Dr-Nayef-AL-RODHAN%20 GCSP]</ref>
In 2009, Al-Rodhan became a Senior Member of [[St. Antony’s College, Oxford]] University, where he analyses, amongst other things, critical turning points in the Arab-Islamic world and their current and future regional and global geopolitical relevance.<ref>[http://www.sant.ox.ac.uk/mec/fellows.html St. Antony’s College, University of Oxford]</ref>
In 2014, he became an Honorary Fellow of St. Antony's College.<ref>[http://www-old.sant.ox.ac.uk/mec/fellows.html St. Anthony's College, University of Oxford]</ref>
His current [[geostrategy]] interests include: [[Geopolitics]] of the [[Middle East]]; sustainable national and global security; geopolitics of [[outer space]] and strategic technologies; and global [[strategic]]   cascading risks.
His [[Philosophy|philosophical]] interests include:[[global justice]]; [[human dignity]] and [[international]] order; shared history of [[human]]ity and [[Transculturation|transcultural]] security and synergy; philosophy of [[sustainable]] history and the [[dignity]] of man; [[history of ideas]]; [[neurophilosophy]] of  [[human nature]] and its implications for war, peace and moral and political [[cooperation]] between [[ideologies]], states and cultures.<ref>[http://www.sustainablehistory.com/nayef-al-rodhan.html Sustainable History]</ref><ref>[http://www.eviangroup.org/events/bio.php?uid=7225%20 The Evian Group]</ref>

'''Theodor Hermann Sternberg''' (January 5, 1878 – April 18, 1950) was a [[German people|German]] [[Jurisprudence|legal philosopher]] serving as a [[O-yatoi gaikokujin|foreign advisor]] in [[Meiji period]] [[Japan]], where he was an important contributor to the development of [[Civil law (legal system)|civil law]] in Japan.

==Biography==
Born in [[Berlin]], Sternberg served as an instructor at [[Tokyo Imperial University]] from 1913 to 1918. He also lectured on occasion at [[Meiji University]], and other major Japanese universities, speaking on civil law, [[criminal law]] and jurisprudence.

He later served as a consultant to the {{nihongo|[[Ministry of Justice (Japan)]]|[[:ja:司法省|司法省]] |''Shihōshō''}} in 1918 and from 1922 to 1925, where he helped oversee the implementation of the Japanese legal codes. He died in [[Tokyo]].

* ''Einführung in die Rechtswissenschaft'', 1912
